
Cybersecurity Incident Response Plan
Organization: Harness Projects
Date: February–March 2025
Category: Incident Response Planning / GRC
Client: Simulated small-business client (5–7 employees)
Role: Solo author / Policy owner


Scenario / Objective
Designed and authored a complete Cybersecurity Incident Response Plan for a simulated small-business client. Served as policy owner and sole author.

Objective: establish structured processes to detect, respond to, and recover from cybersecurity incidents — ensuring business continuity, compliance with regulatory obligations, and protection of customer and corporate data.

Note: Client is fictional. Document uses designed/developed framing. Scenarios assisted by AI for training purposes.


Framework Applied
NIST SP 800-61 — Computer Security Incident Handling Guide
IR Lifecycle: Preparation → Detection & Analysis → Containment → Eradication → Recovery → Post-Incident Activity


Plan Scope
Applies to all employees, contractors, and third-party service providers.

Covered incident types:

Unauthorized access attempts
Data breaches
Phishing and social engineering attacks
Ransomware and malware infections
Denial-of-Service (DoS/DDoS) attacks
Insider threats
Compromised cloud accounts (AWS, Google Workspace)
Unpatched vulnerabilities actively being exploited
Misuse of privileged accounts
Loss or theft of company-owned devices


Key Design Decisions
Severity classification tiers
Defined severity levels (Critical, High, Medium, Low) with clear criteria for each — enabling consistent triage decisions without requiring senior analyst involvement for every alert.

Escalation paths and communication protocols
Documented who gets notified at each severity level, response time expectations, and external communication requirements (customers, regulators, legal).

Regulatory notification obligations
Addressed breach notification requirements and business continuity obligations — scoped appropriately for a small organization without dedicated legal or compliance staff.

Resource-constrained design
Plan explicitly scoped for a 5–7 person organization. Procedures written to be executable by non-security staff in the absence of a dedicated SOC, which required clear language, simple escalation trees, and realistic response expectations.


Why This Matters for SOC Roles
Most IR plans are written for enterprise environments with full security teams. Designing one for a small organization with no dedicated security staff requires understanding both the technical requirements and the operational constraints — which means knowing what to prioritize when resources are limited.
