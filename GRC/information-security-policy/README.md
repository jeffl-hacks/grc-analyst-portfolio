Security Incident Reporting Policy — Deep Dive
As primary author of this policy, key design decisions included:

Incident classification tiers — defined severity levels with clear criteria enabling consistent triage without requiring senior involvement for every event.

Reporting procedures — documented internal escalation paths, external notification timelines (aligned to GDPR's 72-hour breach notification requirement), and communication templates.

Escalation paths — mapped who gets notified at each severity level including legal, compliance, executive leadership, and affected customers.

Regulatory alignment — built GDPR and CCPA breach notification obligations directly into the reporting workflow so compliance is procedural, not reactive.


Analysis
What makes this significant for GRC roles:

Writing 18 sub-policies for a real client requires more than copying templates. Each policy had to be calibrated to the client's actual operating environment — a fully remote company with no physical office, operating across all 50 states, handling sensitive legal data, with GDPR exposure from California users.

The AI Customer Interaction Policy is particularly notable — most organizations don't have one yet. Authoring governance for AI tools handling customer data in a legal context requires understanding both the technical risk and the regulatory landscape around AI and data privacy.
